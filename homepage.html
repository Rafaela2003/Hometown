Location

Faro is located in the southern region of Portugal called the Algarve. It has an estimated population of 61,000 inhabitants since 2019 which makes it the biggest city and second most populous area in the Algarve covering the biggest area.

Weather

Faro has a hot summer Meditteranean climate which means that the summers are generally warm to hot and sunny with average temperatures being 27°C – 35°C that can linger into October. During the winter the weather is mild compared to other European countries with temperatures averaging around 8°C – 16°C in these cold months. It typically rains within these months as well. In recent years the climate has become hotter and rainfall has diminished. Faro is therefore considered to be one of the sunniest cities in Europe.


Transportation

It also has its own International Airport called Gago Countinho Airport also having other means of public transport such as a railway station and minibuses.
